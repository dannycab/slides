# Some things worth complicating

<hr class="rule">

<div class="columns">
<div>

## On the tools

### "AI" is not one thing
Embedding models, fine-tuned classifiers, LLMs doing open-ended generation — **These are technically and epistemically distinct.** Calling all of them "AI" is doing rhetorical work, not methodological work.

### Sprinkling AI gets papers published
The publication economy rewards novelty. **Right now "AI" is the novelty.** That incentive structure should make us suspicious of our own enthusiasm — including mine.

</div>
<div>

## On the traditions

### Qualitative research is not "themes in text"
Interpretivism, grounded theory, phenomenology, discourse analysis - **These traditions carry hard-won epistemological commitments**: that meaning is constructed, not recovered; that the researcher's positionality is data; that reflexivity is not optional.

### Automated classification is not an extension of those traditions
It is a replacement of them, wearing their language. **Calling it qualitative research should require a serious argument** — not an assumption.

</div>
</div>

<div class="bottom">

### These traditions took decades to earn legitimacy in a field that didn't want them.
We should be careful about who gets to claim that legitimacy now, what gets lost in the claiming, and whether the people most invested in these tools are positioned to ask those questions honestly.

</div>
