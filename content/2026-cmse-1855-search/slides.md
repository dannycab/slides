  - Organized for search Spring 2025

- Joint appointment across CMSE and partner units (CSE, STT, CEPSE, PRiME/Create for STEM). 
  - Presumed to be a majority CMSE hire (>50%)

  - Zoom interviews conducted & candidate visits Spring 2026

## Committee & Process Governance

- Search Committee: Caballero (Chair), Green, Kinsmanm, Xie, Yadav (Search Advocate), Zhang
- Process followed the approved **Search Narrative** 
  - Advertising, Requset for Materials
  - Triage, two‑pass screening
  - Remote interviews as needed
  - On‑campus visits, and, now, offer process

---

## Applicant Pool & Triage (with Rubric)

- **Total applications received by deadline:** **35**. 
- **Advanced beyond triage:** **13**.
- **Triage procedure:** at least two reviewers per file; check **minimum required criteria**.

**Triage Criteria (Must‑Have):**  
- Ph.D. earned by Aug 16, 2026.  
- Research in AI, Data Science, and/or Computing Education (or overlapping related post‑secondary discipline).  
- If Ph.D. not in a quantitative/related field, Master’s+ in a quantitative field.  

---

## First‑Pass Screen

- **Inputs read:** cover letter, CV, research statement, teaching statement 
  - Letters and writing sample **not** used at this stage.
- **Reviewers:** two per candidate. 
  - **Scale:** 0/1/2 on five criteria. 
  - **All 13** advanced to second‑pass. 

---

## First‑Pass Screen Rubric

**Criteria and Weights (First‑Pass):**  
- A. Inclusion/Belonging (Required, wt 2.0)  
- B. Scholarly Research (Required, wt 2.0)  
- C. Teaching & Mentoring (Required, wt 2.0)  
- D. Service (Required, wt 1.0)  
- E. Alignment with Target Area (Preferred, wt 1.0)  

**Total possible:** 16
- **Any required criterion with mean score 0 ⇒ overall 0 ⇒ disqualify.**


---

**Scoring summaries (0/1/2) by criterion:**  
- **A (Inclusion):** 2 = clear understanding + track record; 1 = past commitment + plans; 0 = limited appreciation/no plans. 
- **B (Research):** 2 = strong/steady record + clear pathway; 1 = adequate record + clear direction; 0 = weak/incremental.
- **C (Teaching):** 2 = concrete examples/evidence‑based strategies + barriers considered; 1 = interest/awareness + plans; 0 = formulaic/limited awareness. 
- **D (Service):** 2 = strong record + plans; 1 = some efforts + commitment; 0 = limited/no record or generic.  
- **E (Alignment):** 2 = research focuses on target area; 1 = relates/has potential; 0 = limited connection. 

---

## Second‑Pass Screen

- **Inputs read:** cover letter, CV, teaching statement, research statement, **external letters**, **writing sample**. 
- **Reviewers:** three per candidate (plus at least one not on first‑pass).  
- **Scale:** **0–3** per criterion to increase differentiation; half‑scores permitted after committee discussion. 

---


**Criteria (Second‑Pass) & 0–3 anchors:**  
- **A Inclusion (Required, wt 2.0):** 3 = clear understanding + strong track record + potential for leadership; 2 = clear understanding + past commitment; 1 = past commitment + plans; 0 = limited appreciation/no plans. 
- **B Research (Required, wt 2.0):** 3 = strong/steady record, clear pathway, external letters show driving force; 2 = commensurate record; 1 = adequate record/limited funding potential; 0 = weak/incremental. 
- **C Teaching (Required, wt 2.0):** 3 = concrete examples, evidence‑based strategies, barriers addressed, history of inclusive initiatives; 2 = clear interest/experience; 1 = interest + some awareness/plans; 0 = formulaic/limited awareness.

---


**Criteria (Second‑Pass) & 0–3 anchors:**  
- **D Service (Required, wt 1.0):** 3 = established record + concrete plans; 2 = strong record + vague plans; 1 = some efforts; 0 = limited/no efforts. - **E Alignment (Preferred, wt 1.0):** 3 = research **focuses** on target area with critical stance; 2 = focuses with clear potential to improve outcomes; 1 = relates; 0 = limited connection. ## On‑Campus Visit Structure (with Rubric Connections)

- **Talk:** 45–50 minutes + 10 Q&A; accessible; open to units. Evidence contributes to Research, Teaching, Inclusion, Alignment, Service as specified in the Offer‑stage rubric. 
- **Meetings:** Chairs/Director; faculty/staff/students provide feedback via the hiring matrix; Search Committee meets each candidate with pre‑approved questions. 

---

## Offer‑Stage Evaluation

After visits, committee assigns consensus scores (half‑scores allowed) using **same five criteria** (weights unchanged). 

- **Any required criterion = 0 ⇒ disqualify**; sum forms ranked list (or above/below threshold per policy). 

---

**Offer‑Stage Rubric anchors (0/1/2):**  
- **A Inclusion (wt 2.0):** 2 = clear understanding + strong track record; 1 = past commitment + plans; 0 = limited/no plans. Evidence from talk, statements, CV, letters, committee interview, 1‑1s.   
- **B Research (wt 2.0):** 2 = talk advances field for undergrad STEM; strong/steady record; clear pathway; strong connections in meetings; Senior/Junior expectations specified; 1 = considerable progress/adequate record; 0 = unclear/incremental.   
- **C Teaching (wt 2.0):** 2 = very well‑organized talk; concrete teaching examples; evidence‑based strategies; inclusive initiatives; 1 = well organized + awareness/commitment; 0 = difficult to follow/formulaic. Junior/Senior distinctions apply.  

---

**Offer‑Stage Rubric anchors (0/1/2):**  
- **D Service (wt 1.0):** 2 = strong past record + plans; 1 = some efforts + commitment; 0 = limited/generic. Evidence includes talk, letters, CV, committee interview.   
- **E Alignment (wt 1.0):** 2 = research focuses on target area with critical stance; 1 = relates/clear potential; 0 = limited connection. Evidence across talk, pubs, writing, letters, statements, CV, interviews.
